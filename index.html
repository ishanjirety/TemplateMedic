About

With more than two decades of work as a mental health professional with children, youth, families, and adults, I consistently aspire to provide excellent therapeutic alliance and care, enhance recovery and empower my clients to build their resilience.

My journey began when I was offered Psychology as one of the five subjects of study in grade 11 of High school. For the first time in life, I loved studying a subject. I knew then, that all further academic perusal would be in this subject only, and have never regretted any course, or new learning in the subject. Gradually, working in the counseling and clinical field of psychology excited me more, and I completed the Pd.D. in the same field. As soon as a client begins work with me; I am committed to provide a compassionate and non- judgmental space for a collaborative work. A psychotherapy session; where I am completely present in the moment often feels like a meditative state to me. Being able to empower the client with clarity and direction of their mental processes is immensely satisfying to me. I have worked with adolescents, youth, and adults on various psychosocial, relationships, addictions, family disputes, adjustment, mental illness, behavioral issues, suicide risk, etc. The therapeutic modalities that I largely use include cognitive-behavioral therapy, mindfulness, humanistic approach, and relaxation techniques.

My professional life took me to work opportunities in educational institutions; leading me to believe in possibilities of prevention of mental illness through various resilience programs in schools. I therefore, enjoy connecting to parents, administrators and teachers of elementary, High School and even Post-secondary institutes through psychoeducation.

I believe that awareness programs about attachment needs and mental health care during pregnancy can prevent many disorders caused by poor or lack of attachment to parent figures. Similarly, parenting skills training during early childhood, and thereafter each transitional stage of school, can prevent many insecurities and create better parent-child bond resulting in resilient youth. I have been practicing and designing age-appropriate need-based programs wherever I worked and have seen the long term effect of the same. I am convinced by the possibility of building resilience and preventing mental distress through early intervention for parents.
