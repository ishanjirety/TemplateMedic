About

The ups and downs of life sometimes take a toll on us. The routine functioning of day to day activities at home, work or in social circle become a challenge to complete or enjoy. When irrespective of all the efforts to feel better; things continue to bother, it is time for psychotherapy.

I welcome you to the comfort of a safe and trusting place, with listening and understanding through our therapeutic alliance. By working on mutually agreed upon goals and treatment plan, I focus on empowering the client through increased awareness of their inner patterns and find the courage to face problems, regain a sense of purpose.

With extensive experience of individual, group counseling and psycho education, I have worked with children, youth, adults and families for various mental health concerns, issues of adjustment, stress, family conflict, relationship, parenting, academic adjustments and other conflicts. I understand the multicultural dynamics and work with diverse, and immigrant population.

I am committed to provide a compassionate and non- judgmental space for a collaborative work. A recipient of Fulbright Award, I have international exposure to the best practices in the field. A member of various professional associations, I am keen to update my skills and learn about the latest in the field of psychotherapy.

Reaching out to those who need help and spreading the message of mental health care is my mission of life. I strive to contribute through the message of hope and positivism through self-care.

Services

Understandably, clients bring into the session a variety of distressful experiences. Whatever the nature and type of concern, I work towards enabling the client to recognize their strengths and adjust mental processes, behaviors, and thoughts to see things more adaptively and healthily. My practice specializes in treating individuals, couples, and families and I offer comprehensive services dealing with:

Therapy for Anxiety

Is anxiety taking over your life? Does it feel like you can’t control it no matter how hard you try? Have you already tried therapy but found it ineffective?





Therapy for Anxiety

Do you worry a lot? Are the thoughts of fear, and the worst consequences of a situation bothering you? Are these persistent thoughts making it difficult to go through the day’s routine? If any of the answers is yes, therapy may help you. Some of the signs of an anxiety disorder are-

Some of the signs of an anxiety disorder are-

  • • Fear or anxiety is out of proportion to the actual threat.
  • • Difficulty to control the worry.
  • • Feeling restlessness, keyed up, or on edge.
  • • Being easily fatigued.
  • • Difficulty concentrating or mind going blank.
  • • Irritability.
  • • Muscle tensions.
  • • Sleep disturbance (difficulty falling or staying asleep, or restless, unsatisfying sleep).

Therapy for Depression

I want you to know that you are not alone and there are people who can help. Many of my clients find relief in realizing that their struggle does not own them and that there are many options available to once again have hope for their lives.




Therapy for Depression

Do you feel sad and lonely, worthless, or guilty? Are the family and friends sensing agitation in your behavior? Are you finding yourself disinterested in activities, even those that you enjoyed earlier? If any of the answers is yes, therapy may help you.

Some of the signs of depressive disorders are-

  • • Sad, empty, or irritable mood.
  • • Irritability and indecisiveness.
  • • Out of control behavior, or recurrent temper outbursts.
  • • Diminished interest or pleasure in all, or almost all, activities.
  • • Inability to sleep, or oversleeping.
  • • Psychomotor agitation such as feelings of restlessness, or being slowed down.
  • • Fatigue or loss of energy nearly every day.
  • • Feelings of worthlessness or excessive or inappropriate.
  • • Thoughts of death, recurrent suicidal ideation.
  • • Inability to function in our social or work situation.

Couples Therapy

Do you feel like your relationship is at a standstill? Are you and your partner stuck in the same argument loop every night? Do you feel like you and your partner are drifting away? Do you desire more intimacy between you and your partner?




Couples Therapy

Are you and your partner disturbed due to frequent arguments? Do you feel like you and your partner are losing emotional connection and drifting away? Do you desire improved relations between you and your partner? If any of the answers is yes, therapy may help you.

Some of the signs of marital conflicts are-

  • • Criticizing one another.
  • • Withdrawing from one another.
  • • Feeling of contempt and anger for one another.
  • • Poor communication.
  • • Infidelity, or abuse in marriage.

Parental Guidance

Our family affects who we are and who we become, both for the better and for worse. We learn our vocabulary, our habits, our customs and rituals, and how to view and observe the world around us.




Parental Guidance

Are you unable to communicate love and affection to your children? Are you finding it a challenge to understand your teenager’s behavior? Is your child behaving out of control? Would you like to seek parenting guidance and learn about effective bonding with children? If any of the answers is yes, parenting guidance may help you.

Some of the signs of a need for parenting guidance are-

  • • Your child lack self-confidence.
  • • Your child fears failure.
  • • Your child behaves aggressively.
  • • Your child lies a lot.
  • • Your child has low academic performance, or is withdrawn, or is sad.

Stress and Conflict

Has your life been touched by a major event such as a death, divorce or layoffs at work? Are you having a hard time moving through and past the emotional turmoil? Do you feel completely stuck and like you need guidance getting back on track?



Stress and Conflict

Persistent stress affects one's physical and mental health. Lack of harmony and experience of conflicts at the workplace, in the family, or community can cause immense stress and discomfort. Please consider taking psychotherapy if the stress gets overwhelming and affects your personal social or workplace functioning.

Some signs of stress include-

  • • Physical signs- Headaches and body aches, fatigue, digestive problems, muscle tensions, nausea, dizziness, chest pain, rapid heart rate, etc.
  • • Emotional signs- Anger outbursts, irritability, frustration, loss of emotional control or sadness, feeling overwhelmed, or lack of motivation.
  • • Social Signs- social withdrawal, or increase aggression, misinterpreting people, hypersensitive to criticism, argumentative, etc.
  • • Behavioral signs- an inability to rest, drug or alcohol abuse, overeating or undereating, and self-medication.
